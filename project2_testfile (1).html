Example¶

Here is an equation with constants I picked up somewhere. Let's pretend f is foxes, and h is hares.

$\vec{x'} = \begin{pmatrix} \alpha & -\beta f \\ \delta h & -\epsilon \end{pmatrix} \vec{x}$

$\vec{x} = \begin{pmatrix} h \\ f \end{pmatrix}$

$\alpha = .4 \\ \beta = .005 \\ \delta = .005 \\ \epsilon = .2$

I haven't been able to figure out what to do with it, but here's the wronskian:

$\lambda ^2 -0.4\lambda - 0.08 + 0.0003\cdot (f \cdot h) = 0$

These constants give a really nice integer equilibrium solution; by selecting either line (I'll do the first). Letting rate of change be 0:

$\begin{align}0 &= h(\alpha - \beta f) \\ &=\alpha - \beta f \end{align}$

$f= \frac{\alpha}{\beta} \\ r=\frac{\epsilon}{\delta}$

import numpy as np
from scipy.integrate import odeint
import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

def f(s,t):
    a = .01
    b = .01
    d = .01
    e = .01
    fox = s[0]
    hare = s[1]
    dharedt = hare * (a - (b * fox))
    dfoxdt = fox * (((d * hare) - e))
    return [dfoxdt, dharedt]

t = np.linspace(0,3000)

s0=[1,1]  # prey, predator
s1=[0.9,1.1]
s2=[0,1]

sa = odeint(f,s0,t)
sb = odeint(f,s1,t)
sc = odeint(f,s2,t)

plt.plot(t,sa[:,0],'r--', linewidth=2.0)
plt.plot(t,sa[:,1],'b-', linewidth=2.0)
plt.title("equilibrium solution")
plt.xlabel("t")
plt.legend(["Predators","Prey"])
plt.show()

plt.plot(t,sb[:,0],'r--', linewidth=2.0)
plt.plot(t,sb[:,1],'b-', linewidth=2.0)
plt.title("near equilibrium")
plt.xlabel("t")
plt.legend(["Predators","Prey"])
plt.show()

plt.plot(t,sc[:,0],'r--', linewidth=2.0)
plt.plot(t,sc[:,1],'b-', linewidth=2.0)
plt.title("exponential")
plt.xlabel("t")
plt.legend(["Predators","Prey"])
plt.show()
